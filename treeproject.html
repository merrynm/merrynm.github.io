Best Tree Award - Operation Sparty Appleseed

CSE335: Object-Oriented Software Design, Fall 2019, Dr. Charles B. Owen

Winning this programming competition was one of the highlights and proudest moments of my CS undergraduate career. I was one of 10 winners selected for this award out of a class size of nearly 250. This competition is very well-known among CS students at Michigan State, and occurs at the end of every semester of CSE335. These were some of my competitors. (Mine is #1622)

This tree was developed in Microsoft Visual Studio using C++. It is composed of branches at multiple depths, "leaves" (cotton candy), and "fruit" (lollipops) and is designed to grow taller and spawn more branches as the slider is moved to the right. At first it only spawns branches, but once the tree reaches a certain height, it then gains the potential to spawn leaves, and eventually fruit. If the slider is moved back toward the left, the tree will grow in reverse (as demonstrated in the above video). The tree was also designed to "sway" when the wind speed slider is adjusted.

A pseudorandom number generator was incorporated into the project to allow the functionality of seeds, which is also demonstrated above. A random tree can be generated at any time and each seed number corresponds to a unique tree.
